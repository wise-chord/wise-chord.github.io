Material Design is a design system created by Google. It uses grid-based layouts, responsive animations, and depth effects like lighting and shadows to create a clean, intuitive user interface.

## Key Principles

- **Material is the metaphor**: Inspired by the physical world and its textures, surfaces, and shadows
- **Bold, graphic, intentional**: Purposeful use of color, typography, and white space
- **Motion provides meaning**: Smooth transitions guide the user's attention

## Core Components

### Cards

Cards are a fundamental component in Material Design. They display content and actions on a single topic.

### Elevation

Elevation is expressed through shadows. Higher elevation means the element is closer to the user.

### Color

Material Design 3 uses a color system based on a seed color that generates a complete palette of accessible colors.

## Why Use Material Design?

It provides a consistent, predictable, and beautiful foundation for building user interfaces across platforms.
